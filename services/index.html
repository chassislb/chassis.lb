HOW PRICING IS DECIDED

The range is not random. It depends on how much structure the business actually needs.

A focused diagnosis is not priced like a full operational rebuild. A website is not priced like a custom platform. A light partnership is not priced like active weekly correction. During the discovery call, scope is defined based on complexity, urgency, deliverables, technical features, on-site involvement, and the level of responsibility Chassis carries during execution.

FAQ

Before you book a call.

Questions serious clients usually ask before working with Chassis.

What if I am not sure what the actual problem is?

Start with Operational Diagnosis. It identifies whether the issue is in the offer, operations, systems, infrastructure, or decision-making.

Do I need every service?

No. Most clients need one clear starting point. Diagnosis decides whether the next step is structuring, systems, infrastructure, or partnership.

Can we start with one issue only?

Yes. A focused project is often smarter than forcing a large rebuild before the real problem is clear.

Do you only work with hospitality businesses?

No. Hospitality is a strong fit, but Chassis works with any business where unclear structure, weak systems, or messy execution are creating pressure.

Do you build websites without structuring?

Yes, if the business is already clear. If the business is unclear, structure comes first. A website should support the business, not cover confusion.

What happens during a discovery call?

The call identifies the current friction, urgency, and fit. It is used to decide whether the work starts with diagnosis, structuring, implementation, or partnership.

Do you work remotely?

Yes. Structuring, planning, websites, and systems can be handled remotely. On-site observation may be recommended when the issue depends on real daily behavior.

Can projects be phased?

Yes. Larger projects can be split into diagnosis, structure, systems, infrastructure, then partnership if needed.

How long does a typical project take?

It depends on scope. A focused diagnosis may take days. Structuring or infrastructure work usually takes weeks. Operational systems may take longer.

What if the business is still early?

Early businesses can benefit from structure before growth creates pressure. The work is sized according to the stage of the business.
